Methods in Detecting and Characterizing SARS-CoV-2 (COVID-19)

Scientific Paper, Undergraduate Paper
jeffuntalan
August 15, 2021

Introduction

The severe acute respiratory syndrome coronavirus-2 (SARS-CoV-2) is the cause of the most recent public health challenge in the world: the COVID-19 pandemic. The World Health Organization (WHO) declared that it was a pandemic last March 11, 2020 due to the haste and magnitude of its spread, which started as an epidemic in the city of Wuhan in the Hubei province of China (dos Santos, 2020). The virus was traced back to the Huanan South China Seafood Market, where it was hypothesized to have evolved from another coronavirus from bats.

The pandemic has caused a significant change in the way people live and interact with one another. Public places like restaurants, schools, and stores where people used to aggregate were advised against, leaving establishments to either shift to an online platform or close shop. An increased reliance on the internet and online communication platforms was also an effect of the pandemic. Aside from social changes and economic, a lot of people were also infected with the virus, and the complications that came with it.

While we as citizens contribute to the pandemic’s end by keeping within our homes and limiting contact with other people, our medical professionals and laboratory personnel are in close quarters with the virus—detecting, isolating, and purging it to learn more about the virus and end the pandemic.

At present, multiple vaccines developed by different companies are being deployed among the population. Although vaccines provide a definite barrier of protection by preparing our immune system in combating the virus, careful detection and isolation remains one of the key steps in preventing more infections from taking place.

The importance of studying SARS-CoV-2

At the time of writing there are currently 8 developed vaccines for use in achieving herd immunity. Prior to the development of the vaccines, the primary strategy for coping with the spread of SARS-CoV-2 was through rigorous testing and refraining from physical contact. The world was just learning about the disease at that time, and scientists were bent on understanding the virus.

Public knowledge of the disease is important not only because it keeps the people informed of the ongoing pandemic, but also because understanding the concepts surrounding a subject have been proven to illicit protective behaviors from individuals (Miller et al., 2021). For example, having prior knowledge of a disease have been shown to increase handwashing behaviors, which helps in limiting the spread of disease. Of course, developing these behaviors first requires the acknowledgment of the threat of SARS-CoV-2, contrary to the behavior of some activists against wearing masks (such as those in the US).

SARS-CoV-2 Detection methods

During the time when the vaccine was still being developed, it is accepted that the most effective way to control the spread of the virus is by detecting it early (Pokhrel et al., 2020).

Tests for Current Infection

SARS-CoV-2 can be detected by two methods in use today. The first is through viral and antigen tests, while the second is through antibody tests. Viral tests and antigen tests are classified as tests for current infections by the CDC (2021a) since they test for the presence of a viral infection within the body. Viral tests are known as nucleic acid amplification tests (NAATs) since they multiply suspected viral DNA first to better test for the disease. This amplification is done through something like polymerase chain reaction or PCR, transcription mediated amplification (TMA), or helicase-dependent amplification to name a few. Amplification of viral nucleic acids allow for the efficient use of very tiny amounts of obtained sample.

On the other hand, antigen tests look for the presence of a specific viral antigen and not the actual presence of viral nuclei acid. Antigen tests are also very fast and inexpensive, giving them the title of “rapid test”. However, being cheap and fast has its drawbacks. According to the FDA (2020), “Positive results from antigen tests are highly accurate, but there is a higher chance of false negatives, so negative results do not rule out infection.”. Antigen tests utilize mucus samples from the back of the throat whilst NAATs require a nasopharyngeal swab that requires a long swab to be inserted into the nostril to obtain sample from deep inside the nasal cavity.

Test for Past Infection

Now that the two primary tests for current infections are introduced, there also exists a test for confirming past infections. Confirming previous infections can provide good insight in how and where the disease may have spread. These kinds of tests are called serology tests or antibody tests.

Antibody tests require a small amount of blood to be obtained from a patient. This blood will be tested for the presence of antibodies that the body has created to combat SARS-CoV-2. Because the body typically produces antibodies 1-3 weeks after infection (CDC, 2021b), applying antibody tests to anything but testing for past infections will yield erroneous results. Vaccines may also trigger antibody tests, resulting in a false positive.

Self-Tests

A lesser-known testing protocol involves using a self-collection kit or a “self-test”. Self-tests are basically antigen tests that can be administered on oneself, thus reducing the risk of infection by preventing the individual from going out and exposing oneself (Government of Netherlands, n.d.).

Advantages and Disadvantages of each test

NAATs are by far the most reliable way to determine if one has been infected by SARS-CoV-2. They have the highest reliability among the existing SARS-CoV-2 tests, but it also costs the most. Also, albeit the high reliability that NAATs have, they are still not 100% accurate since it can also yield false-negatives or false-negatives due to the variable distribution of the SARS-CoV-2 in the bodies of those infected. It has been proven that the sputum, followed by nasal swabs, yields accurate results. NAATs can also take a significant amount of time to process, more than any other test (Kameswari. S M.P. Brundha, 2020).

Antigen tests have the primary advantage of being cheap and fast, with test results coming out in a span of two minutes to two hours. Accuracy, however, can be less than that of NAATs (Service, 2020). Antigen tests are usually paired with NAATs especially if the result of the antigen test was negative, but the patient is showing symptoms.

Antibody tests or Serological tests as previously mentioned, is used to determine past infections. It is not as costly as NAATs, and the results also arrive quickly, which are some of its advantages. Another thing to note is that antibody tests can be used to determine who are eligible for donating blood containing convalescent plasma (Sidiq et al., 2020).

The Importance of Testing

Testing is not simply about knowing who got infected and who didn’t, because it tells us a lot of information about the state of the pandemic. It is through testing that we can find out which areas of a country have higher cases and needs prompt action to contain the spread. Testing also provides a clear overview of how many active cases there are, thus providing information on the fore coming demand for hospitals and medical supplies such as oxygen. This would also help us prevent going over “the curve”, thus exceeding the maximum occupancy of hospitals which can lead to deaths.

Every person can help end the pandemic by observing proper protocols which are put in place to prevent the further spread of the virus. While it has been a challenging time for all of us, we can help by doing our part and getting tested, informing others, and staying informed.
